Oskar Schindler's Life

  • 1908 - Oskar Schindler is born on April 28 in Zwittau, Austria-Hungary (now Svitavy, Czech Republic).
  • 1928 - He starts working for his father's machinery business in Zwittau.
  • 1935 - Oskar Schindler moves to Krakow, Poland, where he becomes involved in various business ventures.
  • 1939 - With the invasion of Poland by Nazi Germany, Schindler's enamelware and ammunitions factory becomes a lucrative business.
  • 1940 - Schindler's factory begins employing Jewish forced laborers from the Krakow Ghetto.
  • 1944 - Schindler's efforts to save his Jewish workers intensify as he uses his influence and wealth to protect them from deportation to concentration camps.
  • 1945 - Schindler successfully convinces the Nazis to allow him to move his factory and workers to Brünnlitz (now Brněnec, Czech Republic) to avoid their imminent demise.
  • 1945 - Oskar Schindler and his wife, Emilie, are arrested by the Czechoslovak authorities but are later released.
  • 1962 - Oskar Schindler is recognized as one of the Righteous Among the Nations by Yad Vashem for his heroic efforts in saving the lives of over 1,000 Jews during the Holocaust.
  • 1974 - Oskar Schindler passes away on October 9 in Hildesheim, West Germany.
